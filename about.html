Towards the Digital Architectonic is a collection of selected works by Zifeng Guo on computation, architecture, and design.
Dr. Zifeng Guo holds a doctoral degree from ETH Zürich, Switzerland. Previously, he was trained as an architect and computational designer from the Southeast University, China, and was working part-time as a technical consultant at the Kozo Keikaku Engineering Inc. in Tokyo, Japan on developing optimization algorithms for commercial building programs and layouts, and at AESEU in Nanjing, China as a computational designer on rule-based design and digital fabrications. He is currently a researcher at the SHARE lab, University of Florida.
Zifeng Guo’s research focuses on the relation between Computation, Artificial Intelligence and Architecture. His research interest lies on the new paradigm of thinking and modeling that is revealed by the contemporary artificial intelligence. The main idea of his work can be found in the doctoral thesis From Simulation to Synthesis, Architectural Modeling with Context-Based Encoding Using Data-Driven Computational Machines (DOI: 10.3929/ethz-b-000527259) published by ETH Zürich in 2021.
