Gyroid

Surface wall

The first project Reception center of the 2014 Nanjing Youth Olympic was designed by mapping an image to the surface of the building using a reverse process of the cylindrical projections. It was one of our first experiments of using customly developed computer program in real architectural projects. The computer program generated all joint details as well as fabrication drawings within 5 minutes once the image was fed. All envelope components were labelled by the computer program during the entire period of the construction to track their fabrication process and their spatial positions.The envelope was made with aluminum alloy sheets covered with thermal-processed paints.

The second project Gyroid is a landscape structure located at the side of Taihu lake, Yixin, China. It resembles a special type of limestones that serve as the most essential element for Chinese traditional gardens due to their porous features caused by the long-term surging of water. The structure was designed using customly developed computer program which generates the overall shape and computes all the joints and drawings for fabrication and construction. The structure was made by carbon steel sheets covered with thermal-processed paint.

The third project Surface Wall was built for the 60th Anniversary of Nanjing University of Science and Technology in July 2013. It was implemented by the principle of wave interference. The installation was made by stainless steel components that were laser-cut from fabrication drawings generated by customly wrote computer programs. The steel components were post-processed with thermal-processed paints and were manually assembled.
