The CAAD elective course CAAD-Praxis: Object Localization for Semantic Maps in Urban Design digs into the abundance of urban aerial imageries to create personal semantic maps of any city using object detections and localizations.

CAAD-Praxis: Object Localization for Semantic Maps in Urban Design introduces Convolutional Neural Networks, the state-of-the-art Artificial Intelligence techniques for image processing, to localize specific objects from satellite, aerial, and street view imageries of cities. The identified objects are projected back to space and rendered as semantic maps.

CAAD-Praxis: Object Localization for Semantic Maps in Urban Design doesn not restrict the type of objects being detected, and what is at the stake depends on the interests and questions of each student.
